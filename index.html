⛰️Reach the Summit
Yushan Main Peak
📖 Did You Know?

Taiwan's Jade Mountain

Yushan (Jade Mountain) rises to 3,952 meters (12,966 feet). It is Taiwan's highest peak and the tallest in Northeast Asia.

Many of Taiwan's mountain routes, including Yushan, require permits to help manage access. Some popular routes and mountain huts are assigned by lottery about a month in advance.

Hehuanshan (Hehuan Mt.)
📖 Did You Know?

The Baiyue Challenge

Taiwan has 268 peaks above 3,000 meters (nearly 10,000 feet), packing serious mountain terrain into a small island. The most iconic hiking goal is the Baiyue, Taiwan's Hundred Peaks, a celebrated checklist of 100 signature summits.

From knife-edge ridgelines to wide-open alpine views, every peak feels like a new chapter. Choose your first Baiyue summit and kick off your Taiwan adventure.

Yushan

🎉 Correct! At 3,952m, Yushan stands taller than any peak in Japan, Korea, or Southeast Asia. And yes, it is a hike you can actually do.

How many peaks in Taiwan exceed 3,000 meters?

Hint: Taiwan packs serious altitude into a small island. ⛰️
🎉 Correct! Taiwan's 268 peaks above 3,000m make it one of the most mountainous islands on Earth.

🚲Ride Around the Island
Xinwei Scenic Bridge, Kaohsiung
📖 Did You Know?

Taiwan's Cycling Route No. 1

The route circles the entire island, roughly 960 km (about 600 miles) of dedicated bike paths along the coastline, through rice paddies, past hot springs, and over mountain terrain.

Riders typically complete it in 9 to 12 days. The route is fully sign-posted with rest stops and bike repair stations every few kilometers.

Sun Moon Lake, Nantou
📖 Did You Know?

Sun Moon Lake: A World-Class Cycling Route

The roughly 30 km (18-mile) loop around Sun Moon Lake was named one of the world's top 10 most beautiful bike paths by CNN Travel. The lakeside path hugs the shoreline through forests, passing indigenous Thao villages and grand temples.

Every autumn, the Sun Moon Lake Come! Bikeday festival draws thousands of cyclists.

🎉 Correct! One of the world's great cycling circuits, fully sign-posted with rest stops the whole way. One island, one ride, total freedom.
Sun Moon Lake, Nantou

Ride to Wuling, Hehuanshan

The Taiwan KOM (King of the Mountain) Challenge is one of the world's toughest single-day cycling races. How high does the route climb?

Taiwan: A Runner's Paradise

Taiwan has one of the highest densities of running events in the world, hosting everything from World Athletics Gold Label road races to mountain ultras and island-hopping marathons.

🏄Dive Into the Blue
Green Island
📖 Did You Know?

Taiwan: An Island of Water Adventures

Surrounded by ocean, Taiwan offers water sports year-round. Snorkel with green sea turtles at Xiaoliuqiu, dive among coral reefs off Green Island and Lanyu, surf Pacific swells along the east coast, or join a whale watching tour out of Hualien.

Lanyu
📖 Did You Know?

Lanyu: Where Ocean Culture Runs Deep

Lanyu (Orchid Island), off Taiwan's southeast coast, is home to the indigenous Tao people, who have maintained their ocean-going canoe culture and flying fish traditions for centuries.

The island also offers world-class diving with visibility that often exceeds 30 meters (about 100 feet).

Xiaoliuqiu
QUESTION 1 OF 3

Xiaoliuqiu, a small coral island off Taiwan's southwest coast, is nicknamed "Green Turtle Paradise." Roughly how many green sea turtles live permanently around the island?

Hint: You are practically guaranteed to see them while snorkeling, any time of year!
🎉 Correct! Xiaoliuqiu has one of the highest concentrations of green sea turtles in the world, and it is just a 30-minute ferry ride from Pingtung.
Dapeng Bay
QUESTION 2 OF 3

Dapeng Bay in Pingtung is one of Taiwan's top destinations for sailing and yacht experiences. What makes its waters uniquely suited for water sports?

Hint: The bay has only one narrow inlet connecting it to the open sea!
🎉 Correct! Dapeng Bay's sheltered lagoon creates ideal conditions for sailing, SUP, and yacht cruises. Every spring, the Dapeng Bay Marine Festival fills the bay with hundreds of sails.
Dolphin and Whale Watching in Yilan
QUESTION 3 OF 3

Which of the following water activities can you do in Taiwan?

Hint: Taiwan is surrounded by ocean, and locals have made the most of every stretch of it!
🎉 Correct! From surfing and whale watching to sailing and snorkeling with sea turtles, Taiwan's coastline offers water adventures in every direction.

🧗Chase the Thrill
Paragliding, Yilan
📖 Did You Know?

Taiwan's Outdoor Playground

Beyond hiking and water sports, Taiwan is packed with outdoor adventures for every comfort level. Float above the East Rift Valley in a hot air balloon, raft the rapids of Hualien's Xiuguluan River, paddleboard across Sun Moon Lake at sunrise, or paraglide over rice fields in Taitung.

Taitung Hot Air Balloon Festival
📖 Did You Know?

The Taitung Hot Air Balloon Festival

Every summer, the Luye Highland in Taitung hosts the Taiwan International Balloon Festival, one of the world's top 12 balloon festivals as named by Travel Channel.

Over a million visitors come each year for tethered rides, special-shaped balloons from around the globe, and night glow concerts where balloons light up in sync with music after dark.

Xiuguluan River
QUESTION 1 OF 3

Hualien's Xiuguluan River is Taiwan's most popular white water rafting destination. What makes this river geographically unique?

Hint: The scenery along the route exists because of how this river carved its path to the Pacific!
🎉 Correct! This three-to-four-hour rafting trip takes you through steep canyon walls with a mix of calm stretches and exciting rapids. Accessible for beginners.
Twin Candlesticks, North Coast and Guanyinshan National Scenic Area

Stand-up paddleboarding (SUP) has become one of Taiwan's most popular outdoor activities. Which of these is a favorite SUP spot?

Hint: From calm mountain lakes to dramatic ocean cliffs, Taiwan has a SUP spot for every vibe!
🎉 Correct! Taiwan's diverse geography means SUP ranges from glassy lake surfaces to paddling beneath some of Asia's tallest sea cliffs.
Rock Climbing, Longdong, North Coast and Guanyinshan National Scenic Area

🎉 Correct! River tracing combines hiking, swimming, bouldering, and waterfall climbing as you move upstream through a canyon. Hualien is the top destination, with routes carved through marble gorges near Taroko.

🌲National Parks
Taroko National Park
📖 Did You Know?

Nine National Parks on One Small Island

Taiwan has nine national parks, each protecting a completely different landscape: volcanic fumaroles, marble gorges, high-altitude forests, coral atolls, and coastal wetlands.

Endemic species like the Formosan black bear and the Taiwan blue magpie call these parks home.

Taijiang National Park
📖 Did You Know?

Nature, Closer Than You Think

Many of Taiwan's most dramatic landscapes are surprisingly easy to reach. Yangmingshan National Park, with its steaming volcanic fumaroles and hot springs, sits just minutes from downtown Taipei by bus.

Kenting on the southern tip offers tropical coastline and coral reefs within a short drive from Kaohsiung.

Yangmingshan National Park
QUESTION 1 OF 3

Yangmingshan National Park sits on a volcanic landscape just outside Taipei. What can you still see there today from its volcanic origins?

Hint: The Datun Volcanic Group may be dormant, but the earth beneath your feet is very much alive!
🎉 Correct! Yangmingshan's fumaroles and hot springs are proof that the volcanoes are only sleeping. One of the few places where you can go from a capital city to steaming sulfur vents in under an hour.
Taiwan Blue Magpie
QUESTION 2 OF 3

Taiwan is home to several endemic species found nowhere else on Earth. Which of these animals is protected within Taiwan's national parks?

Hint: Taiwan's parks protect creatures from the mountains to the rivers!
🎉 Correct! Taiwan's national parks preserve habitats for species found nowhere else, from the Formosan black bear in Yushan to the landlocked salmon in Shei-Pa's cold mountain streams.
Shei-Pa National Park
QUESTION 3 OF 3

How many national parks does Taiwan have?

Hint: From coral atolls in the South China Sea to volcanic peaks above Taipei, the range is extraordinary!
🎉 Correct! Nine national parks protect everything from coral atolls to marble gorges to alpine peaks. Taiwan packs more ecological diversity into its parks than countries many times its size.

🚄Ride the Rails
East Longitudinal Valley
📖 Did You Know?

A Train Ride Through Every Landscape

Taiwan's rail network connects nearly every corner of the island. High Speed Rail whisks you from Taipei to Kaohsiung in around 1.5 hours. The round-island railway hugs the dramatic east coast, where mountains drop straight into the Pacific.

And heritage lines like the Alishan Forest Railway climb through cloud forests into breathtaking mountain scenery.

Railway Bento (Lunchbox)
📖 Did You Know?

The Railway Bento

No Taiwan train ride is complete without a railway bento, the classic boxed meal sold at stations and onboard trains across the island.

Some stations have their own regional specialties, and loyal fans debate which one makes the best version.

Taiwan High Speed Rail
QUESTION 1 OF 3

Taipei and Kaohsiung are about 350 km (around 200 miles) apart. How quickly does the High Speed Rail connect Taiwan's two largest cities?

Hint: These trains run at up to 300 km/h (186 mph)!
🎉 Correct! The HSR makes day trips between north and south Taiwan effortless, with trains departing every 15 to 30 minutes throughout the day.
Alishan Forest Railway
QUESTION 2 OF 3

The Alishan Forest Railway uses a rare engineering technique to climb steep mountain slopes. What is it?

Hint: The train zigzags its way up, changing direction at each turn!
🎉 Correct! These switchbacks carry the narrow-gauge train from 30 meters to over 2,200 meters, passing through four distinct climate zones in a single ride.
Sky Lanterns
QUESTION 3 OF 3

The Pingxi Branch Line is one of Taiwan's most scenic short railway rides. What is the line's most famous attraction?

Hint: Thousands of glowing wishes float into the sky here every night!
🎉 Correct! The Pingxi Line winds through old mining villages, but the sky lantern tradition is what draws visitors from around the world. Write a wish, light it up, and let it go.

🏙️Old Streets, New Cities
Dihua St, Taipei
📖 Did You Know?

Where Centuries Layer on Top of Each Other

Taiwan's cities are not just modern skylines. Wander down an old street in Lukang, Jiufen, or Anping in Tainan, and you are walking through centuries of trade, immigration, and craftsmanship.

Songshan Cultural Park, Taipei
📖 Did You Know?

From Factories to Creative Spaces

Across Taiwan, abandoned industrial sites have been reborn as cultural hubs. Huashan 1914 in Taipei was a century-old distillery, now filled with galleries and indie shops. Songshan Cultural Park was a former tobacco factory, now a design hub.

Pier-2 in Kaohsiung turned harbor warehouses into one of Asia's most vibrant art districts.

Anping Old Fort, Tainan
QUESTION 1 OF 3

Tainan is considered Taiwan's oldest city. When did its recorded urban history begin?

Hint: The Dutch East India Company built a trading fort here long before Taipei even existed!
🎉 Correct! Tainan served as Taiwan's capital for over 200 years and is home to the island's first Confucian temple, first Taoist temple, and over 300 historic sanctuaries.
Jiufen, New Taipei City
QUESTION 2 OF 3

Jiufen in New Taipei City is one of Taiwan's most visited old streets. What transformed this quiet mountain village into a boomtown in the late 1800s?

Hint: The mountain was hiding a fortune underground!
🎉 Correct! Jiufen's gold rush legacy lives on in its lantern-lit alleyways, hillside tea houses, and stunning ocean views that draw visitors from around the world.
Pier-2 Art District, Kaohsiung
QUESTION 3 OF 3

Which of these is a real example of Taiwan turning old buildings into creative spaces?

Hint: Taiwan has a talent for giving old places new life!
🎉 Correct! From distilleries to warehouses to factories, Taiwan turns its industrial past into creative spaces that are now among the island's top destinations.

🪷Faith on Every Corner
City God (Chenghuang) Temple, Hsinchu
📖 Did You Know?

An Island of Ten Thousand Temples

Taiwan has over 12,000 registered temples. Taoist, Buddhist, and folk religion temples stand on nearly every block, many centuries old and still buzzing with daily worship.

Incense, offerings, and fortune-telling rituals are not relics of the past. They are everyday life.

Baishatun Mazu Pilgrimage
📖 Did You Know?

The Mazu Pilgrimage

Every spring, millions join the Dajia Mazu Pilgrimage, a nine-day, 340 km (about 200 miles) procession honoring the sea goddess Mazu. Entire towns set up free food stations, and strangers open their homes to walkers.

It has been compared to the Hajj and the Camino de Santiago.

Beigang Chaotian Temple, Yunlin

What can visitors do at most Taiwanese temples?

🎉 Correct! Temples are free to enter, and every visit is a mix of ritual, art, and atmosphere.
Longshan Temple, Taipei

Longshan Temple, built in 1738, is one of Taipei's most famous landmarks. Where is it located?
