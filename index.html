⛰️Reach the Summit
Yushan Main Peak
📖 Did You Know?

Taiwan's Jade Mountain

Yushan (Jade Mountain) rises to 3,952 meters (12,966 feet). It is Taiwan's highest peak and the tallest in Northeast Asia.

Many of Taiwan's mountain routes, including Yushan, require permits to help manage access. Some popular routes and mountain huts are assigned by lottery about a month in advance.

Hehuanshan (Hehuan Mt.)
📖 Did You Know?

The Baiyue Challenge

Taiwan has 268 peaks above 3,000 meters (nearly 10,000 feet), packing serious mountain terrain into a small island. The most iconic hiking goal is the Baiyue, Taiwan's Hundred Peaks, a celebrated checklist of 100 signature summits.

From knife-edge ridgelines to wide-open alpine views, every peak feels like a new chapter. Choose your first Baiyue summit and kick off your Taiwan adventure.

🚲Ride Around the Island
Xinwei Scenic Bridge, Kaohsiung
📖 Did You Know?

Taiwan's Cycling Route No. 1

The route circles the entire island, roughly 960 km (about 600 miles) of dedicated bike paths along the coastline, through rice paddies, past hot springs, and over mountain terrain.

Riders typically complete it in 9 to 12 days. The route is fully sign-posted with rest stops and bike repair stations every few kilometers.

Sun Moon Lake, Nantou
📖 Did You Know?

Sun Moon Lake: A World-Class Cycling Route

The roughly 30 km (18-mile) loop around Sun Moon Lake was named one of the world's top 10 most beautiful bike paths by CNN Travel. The lakeside path hugs the shoreline through forests, passing indigenous Thao villages and grand temples.

Every autumn, the Sun Moon Lake Come! Bikeday festival draws thousands of cyclists.
